All About Penguins! (change when needed)

Penguins are a group of aquatic, flightless birds found primarily in the Southern Hemisphere. They are known for their distinctive black and white plumage and their unique social behaviors.

There are about 18 different species of penguins, ranging from the small Little Blue Penguin to the large Emperor Penguin. Penguins are well adapted to life in the water, with their streamlined bodies and flipper-like wings that allow them to swim gracefully.

These birds primarily feed on fish, krill, and other marine organisms. Their hunting skills are remarkable, often diving to great depths to catch their prey.

Penguins are also known for their strong social bonds. They often huddle together to keep warm in harsh weather conditions, showcasing their unique family structures.
